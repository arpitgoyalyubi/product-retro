Executive Summary

What we shipped

14 releases across 5 quarters — spanning identity automation, bank verification, mobile UX, in-house demat, server-side rendering, and KYC compliance.

What moved

KYC throughput 4.3×. Bank verification ops load cut 3.5×. Demat accounts 8.6×. Web impressions 32×. 24,865 users adopted biometric sign-in.

What it unlocked

Aspero's own DP, Google indexing, KRA V2 recovery path, compliance-grade KYC with wet-sig + nominee — and the infrastructure for FY27 growth.

Platform Critical Mar 10, 2026
Aspero In-House Demat (Own DP)
Own DP + flow reorder 2.4×'d Signup→Demat conversion (3.8% → 9.1%)
3.8%→9.1%
Signup → Demat conversion (2.4×)
8.6×
Demat throughput growth

Aspero launched its own Depository Participant and made demat a native in-flow KYC step. Signup→Demat conversion 2.4×'d (3.8%→9.1%, equal 28-day cohorts). The entire gain is in the PAN→Demat leg (21%→56%) — signup→PAN was unchanged, so it's the in-house DP + flow reorder, not top-of-funnel.

  • Before: demat required a handoff to a third-party DP — external dependency, friction, failure modes; only 21% of PAN-verified users reached demat.
  • After: Aspero's own DP makes demat a seamless native step — 56% of PAN-verified users now reach demat.
  • Signup→Demat conversion: 3.8% (Feb, 3rd-party DP) → 9.1% (May, in-house DP) = 2.4×, +5.3pp.
  • Signup→PAN was flat (17.7%→16.1%) — the improvement is entirely in the back half of KYC where demat sits.
  • Throughput step-change at Mar 10: 66/wk → 190 (first full week) → 600+/wk by Jun 2026 (8.6×); 5,936 accounts post-launch. Clean signal — no marketing change at same time.

Feature Very High Apr 20, 2026
Biometric Authentication
Relogins from signed-up users +143% — biometric was additive, not cannibalistic
+143%
Weekly relogins from signed-up users
24,865
Users with biometric enabled

Fingerprint + face ID as an alternative to PIN. Total weekly relogins from signed-up users more than doubled (1,908 → 4,637/wk, +143%). PIN relogins held steady while biometric added a net-new stream — biometric is already ~51% of all returning-user sign-ins.

  • Before: returning users re-authenticated with PIN only (~1,908/wk). After: PIN or one-tap biometric.
  • Total weekly relogins: 1,908/wk → 4,637/wk (+143%) — more than doubled in ~10 weeks.
  • Additive, not cannibalistic: PIN relogins held steady (~1,908 → ~2,222/wk) while biometric added a net-new ~2,415/wk stream.
  • Adoption: 24,865 users enrolled; biometric now ~51% of all returning-user sign-ins (0 → 2,610/wk).
  • Sign-in time: sub-second for biometric vs 3–5s for PIN + OTP path.

Feature Very High Jun 3, 2026
KRA V2 — CVL Upload Path
KRA-gate drop-off cut from 22.2% → ~6.9% — 230 lost users unblocked, ₹44.3L GTV recovered
22.2%→6.9%
Net KRA-gate drop-off (−15.3pp)
₹44.3L
GTV recovered in 4 weeks

Added a CVL document upload path for users who fail the KRA eligibility gate. Net drop-off at the gate fell from 22.2% (permanent hard-block) to ~6.9% — a 69% reduction, still falling as pending uploads resolve. 69.1% of routed users now unblocked; instant validation improving 13% → 39% as the CVL pipeline warms.

  • Before: 22.2% of signups hit a hard KRA block with zero resolution — permanently lost at the gate.
  • After: every blocked user routed to CVL upload → webhook validation, cutting net KRA-gate drop-off to ~6.9% (−15.3pp, −69%).
  • 333 users entered CVL path. 230 unblocked (69.1%), 25 failed (7.5%), 78 in webhook queue (23.4%, still resolving).
  • Instant validation rate improving week-over-week: 13% → 39% as CVL pipeline warms. P50 webhook turnaround: 14.2h.
  • Trade conversion from unblocked cohort: 22.4% vs 28% baseline — gap narrowing as cohort matures.
